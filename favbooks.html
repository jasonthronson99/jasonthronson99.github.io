My Favorite Books:

Book 1: Python Crash Course

Book 1 Reasoning

When people have told me that they have a hard time with learning Python, I was really confused. I remember struggling for a little bit myself, but one day it became a breeze. I realized that this was because they haven't heard about this book. Now I know that one aspect of programming is the actual problem solving part, and yes that's very difficult, but in terms of learning the syntax, this book makes python extremely easy. I honestly think that it's the definitive beginner resource (or at least the first few chapters are, since I remember the project part being annoying).

Book 2: The Illustrated Happiness Trap

Book 2 Reasoning

So I feel like there are a lot of books on happiness nowadays, and that's great. However I've noticed that they tend to run into two issues: they're either too boring/long, which makes reading them not worth it in my opinion, or they're not really based on any science. This book fixes that by providing science-based advice for happiness (known as acceptance commitment therapy, great stuff), while also providing lots of illustrations to make understanding the material fun, and making it easier to get through.

Book 3: Atomic Habits

Everyone needs to read this book. This book is one of the books that changed my life so much.
